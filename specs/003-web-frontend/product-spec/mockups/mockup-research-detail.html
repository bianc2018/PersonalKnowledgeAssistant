Self-hosting LLMs on consumer GPUs

Completed 14 sources · Generated yesterday · 2,400 words

Executive summary

Running large language models locally is now practical on mid-tier consumer GPUs with 8–16 GB VRAM. The key is picking the right quantization format, inference engine, and model size for your hardware.

Recommended stack

  • llama.cpp for GGUF models on CPU / GPU hybrid.
  • vLLM or TGI for high-throughput serving on NVIDIA GPUs.
  • Ollama for a simple local API with model management.

VRAM rules of thumb

A 7B model at Q4_K_M needs roughly 4.5 GB VRAM for the weights, plus overhead for context and KV cache. Plan for ~6–7 GB at 4k context.

Trade-offs

Lower-bit quantization saves memory but can degrade instruction-following and math performance. Q5_K_M is often the sweet spot for reasoning tasks, while Q4_K_M is fine for summarization and chat.
